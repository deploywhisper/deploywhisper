# DeployWhisper Code of Conduct

DeployWhisper contributors, maintainers, users, and reviewers are expected to keep project spaces respectful, constructive, and focused on improving deployment safety.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful of different backgrounds, experience levels, and operational contexts.
- Assume good intent, but prioritize clarity when production safety, security, or privacy is involved.
- Critique ideas, code, documentation, and evidence without attacking people.
- Keep discussions grounded in reproducible behavior, requirements, tests, benchmarks, or clearly labeled opinions.
- Respect private infrastructure details, incident information, credentials, and organization-specific context.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or sustained disruption.
- Discriminatory language or conduct.
- Publishing private security reports, credentials, incident details, or personal information without permission.
- Misrepresenting project maturity, adoption, safety guarantees, or maintainer decisions.
- Pressuring maintainers or contributors to prioritize private interests over public project health.

## Reporting

For security-sensitive conduct issues, follow `SECURITY.md` when disclosure could expose vulnerabilities, credentials, or private infrastructure details.

For other conduct issues, contact the repository maintainers through the project's public issue or discussion channels when safe to do so. If a public report would expose sensitive information, use a private maintainer contact path listed by the repository owner or CODEOWNERS.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove comments, close issues, restrict participation, or escalate to hosting-platform moderation when behavior harms the project or its participants. Enforcement should be proportional, documented where appropriate, and focused on protecting contributors and users.

## Attribution

This code of conduct is intentionally concise and project-specific. Future governance work may replace it with a formally adopted community code of conduct.
